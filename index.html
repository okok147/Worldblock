Worldblock extends blockchain coordination beyond finance into knowledge sharing, marketplace exchange, resource rental, and local community execution.

System

Two maps turn a global problem into a readable surface.

Worldblock makes need and capacity visible at the same time, so coordination happens against actual resources instead of isolated market signals.

01

Issue Map

Tracks unmet needs, local bottlenecks, and intervention pressure so communities can see where action matters.

02

Real-World Resources Map

Shows where equipment, services, knowledge, spaces, and capital already exist and can be routed.

03

Shared Exchange Layer

Lets proposals, rentals, service transactions, and learning move through one trust-aware protocol surface.

04

Contribution-Based Governance

Keeps voting power close to participation, not pure capital, so reputation compounds into usable civic trust.

Economy

Utility and trust are separated on purpose.

The protocol uses one token to move value and another to anchor reputation, governance, and counterparty filtering.

$WBC

Coordination and settlement

Funds initiatives, pays for services, and carries exchange across the Worldblock marketplace.

$WBV

Soul-bound voting power

Signals contribution, strengthens governance, and helps users identify more trustworthy participants.

Adaptive

Supply that responds to activity

WBC is described with an adaptive supply mechanism that adjusts as network demand expands.

stETH

Financial launch backing

The initial supply concept is financially backed by stETH to establish a more stable starting base.

Applications

Designed for everyday coordination, not abstract governance.

Worldblock is meant to land in practical workflows where trust, logistics, and exchange already shape daily decisions.

01

Decentralized knowledge system

Open educational content, collaborative self-learning, and contributor recognition through trust signals.

02

Business exchange for services and products

Native settlement for offerings, with WBV helping surface more reliable providers and higher-quality supply.

03

Resource rental marketplace

Tools, equipment, and spaces can be listed and rented with clearer counterparty trust.

04

Skill sharing and freelance work

Professionals and hobbyists exchange labor with tokenized settlement and contribution-based endorsements.

05

Local community projects

Neighborhood initiatives, environmental efforts, and cultural programs can be proposed, funded, and managed in public.

Build

Coordinate globally. Exchange locally. Learn in public.

Worldblock treats the planet as a shared operating surface for issues, resources, and trust.
